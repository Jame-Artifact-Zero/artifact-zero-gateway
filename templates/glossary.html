Tilt Taxonomy

10 categories of structural communication drift. Each maps to a Plutchik emotion family — the color tells you which family before you read a word.

T1 Reassurance Drift
Comfort language replaces factual resolution. The reader feels better without knowing more.
"Don't worry, we're handling it." → What specifically? By when? Who owns it?
don't worryit's fineit's okayyou got thisrest assured
T2 Certainty Inflation
Absolute guarantees without enforcement mechanism. Confidence exceeds what the system can deliver.
"Our platform guarantees 100% uptime." → Without SLA enforcement, this is just a feeling.
guarantee100%zero riskperfectalways
T3 Consensus Claims
Manufactured agreement without evidence. Invokes a crowd that was never polled.
"Most people agree this is the right approach." → Which people? What data?
most peopleeveryoneno onetypicallyin general
T4 Capability Overreach
Claims of universal capability without boundary acknowledgment. Everything is covered. Until it isn't.
"Our solution handles everything end-to-end." → What are the edges? What does it NOT do?
solves everythingany scenariouniversal solutionworks for everyone
T5 Absolute Language
Binary framing that eliminates nuance. Always/never constructions that deny edge cases.
"We never miss a deadline." → Demonstrably false for any organization of sufficient size.
alwaysnevercompletelyentirelyeveryone
T6 Constraint Deferral
Hard requirements pushed to future without assignment or timeline. Abandoned in slow motion.
"We'll figure that out in phase 2." → Phase 2 has no date, no owner, no definition.
latereventuallyphase 2future iterationwe'll figure it out
T7 Category Blend
Distinct concepts merged into vague composite. "Basically" is the sound of precision dying.
"Basically, it's kind of the same thing overall." → Three blend markers. Nothing was said.
kind ofsort ofbasicallyoverallat the end of the day
T8 Pressure Optimization
Artificial urgency to force decisions before evaluation is complete. Time pressure replaces readiness.
"We need this ASAP — confirm today?" → Why today? What changes if we wait 24 hours?
nowtodayASAPimmediatelyright away
T9 Scope Expansion
Response introduces domain tokens not present in prompt. Conversation silently migrates to new territory.
Ask about database optimization → get advice about team hiring. Where did that come from?
domain token analysis>55% new tokens

Unnamed experts invoked to end discussion. Authority cited without source. Says "stop questioning" without saying it.
"Research shows this is best practice." → Which research? Published where? Sample size?

Parent Failure Modes

Three structural failures that cascade through communication layers. Each breaks integrity differently. CCA is the most dangerous due to invisibility.

FM-01 UDDS — Unresolved Dependency Drift via Substitution
A constraint declared at L0 is replaced at L2 with a structurally different substitute that appears to satisfy it but doesn't. The original constraint is never violated — it's bypassed.
"We addressed your concern" — but with something else entirely.
Derivative Failures
DF-01 Substitution CascadeEach substitute introduces a new gap requiring its own substitute.
DF-02 Boundary ErosionRepeated substitution softens hard constraints into preferences.
DF-03 Confidence DisplacementConfidence in substitute mistaken for resolution of original.
DF-04 False ResolutionSubstitute closes loop perceptually while constraint remains unmet.
FM-02 DCE — Deferred Constraint Erosion
A constraint acknowledged at declaration time is systematically deferred until it becomes structurally invisible. Not denied — delayed into irrelevance.
"We'll handle that later" — but later never arrives.
Derivative Failures
DF-01 Constraint SurpriseDeferred constraint surfaces at production, causing late-stage failure.
DF-02 Patch DependenceSystem relies on future fixes to justify present decisions.
DF-03 Scope Deferral JustificationConstraint excluded via "out of scope" language.
DF-04 Late-Stage BlameResponsibility shifted to users when deferred constraint surfaces.
FM-03 CCA — Constraint Collapse via Aggregation
Multiple distinct constraints merged into composite representation, losing enforcement precision. Aggregates look compliant while localized failures go silent. Most dangerous mode due to invisibility.
"Taken together, these constraints balance out." — Individual failures are invisible.
Derivative Failures
DF-01 Constraint AveragingHard constraints reduced to mean behavior. Individual precision lost.
DF-02 Edge DilutionExtreme but valid constraints dismissed as outliers.
DF-03 Composite Safety IllusionSystem appears robust because aggregates look acceptable.
DF-04 Silent Constraint ViolationIndividual failures masked by overall success metrics.
Interaction Matrix
UDDS + DCESubstitution enables deferral. The substitute is accepted as "good enough" so the real constraint gets pushed to "later."
UDDS + CCASubstitution hides inside aggregation. Individual substitutes invisible when only averages are checked.
DCE + CCADeferral rationalized by aggregation. "Overall we're on track" masks that 3 constraints have been pushed.
UDDS + DCE + CCA (TRIADIC)Maximum cascade. Constraint substituted → substitute deferred → deferral hidden by aggregate metrics. Undetectable without layer analysis.

Layer Model

NTI evaluates communication across 8 structural layers. Failures propagate upward — a crack at L0 becomes invisible by L5.

LayerNameDescription
L0 Reality Substrate Hard constraints from the physical world. Budgets, deadlines, regulations, physics. Cannot be negotiated — only acknowledged or ignored.
L1 Input Freeze Stated objective at time of declaration. Once frozen, original intent preserved regardless of downstream interpretation.
L2 Interpretive Framing Where constraints get reworded, softened, or reframed. Hedge markers, reassurance, and category blending happen here. Primary mutation layer.
L3 Objective Integrity Similarity between declared objective and downstream execution. Jaccard similarity, drift scoring, mutation marker detection.
L4 Execution Vectors Actions taken vs. declared. Misalignment means the plan existed but execution diverged.
L5 Output Enforcement Final output checked against original L0 constraints. Drift modes flagged. Where failure becomes visible — or doesn't.
L6 Interface Contracts JOS binding layer. Operator decisions cannot be overridden by system interpretation. Act / Park / Discard — no fourth option.
L7 Telemetry Audit trail. Request IDs, timestamps, latency, processing metadata. Forensic review layer.

Definitions

Every acronym, code, and term used across the NTI system. Color-coded to the emotion family it belongs to.

CORE SYSTEM
NTI No-Tilt Interface
Rule-based AI governance system that enforces structural integrity in human-AI communications. Detects and classifies communication failures without requiring LLM inference.
NII Narrative Integrity Index
0-100 score measuring how structurally sound a piece of communication is. Higher = more integrity. Computed from constraint preservation, tilt density, and failure mode presence. The single number that answers "is this text trustworthy?"
JOS Jame's Operating System
Containment architecture with 5 fields: Objective, Constraints, No-Go Zones, Definition of Done, Closure Authority. Prevents scope drift and ensures operator decisions cannot be overridden by system interpretation. Act/Park/Discard — no fourth option.
AZ-CCS Artifact Zero Communication Color Standard
Formal specification mapping Plutchik's 8 primary emotions to NTI structural failure categories via color. Eight permanent colors with three intensity tiers (glow, signal, deep). The standard that defines how communication intent is visually encoded.
